h1. Ruby's Regexp.union

I can across the a handy regular expression method in Ruby the other day. It's called "Regexp.union":http://www.ruby-doc.org/core-1.9.3/Regexp.html#method-c-union and can be used as follows:

<pre data-language="ruby">
  regexs = [
    /^layout: .+$/,
    /^title: .+$/
  ]

  contents = [
    'layout: layout-to-be-removed',
    'title: Some title that will be removed',
    'tags: this, will, be, kept'
  ]

  r = Regexp.union(regexs)

  p contents.reject { |line| line.match(r) }
</pre>

Produces:

<pre data-language="ruby">
  ["tags: this, will, be, kept"]
</pre>
